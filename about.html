About Me


I've been interested in technology since I was a little kid, and started programming for the first time on an old Macintosh iMac G3 in AppleScript, making scripts to empty the computer's trash, and even created a simple choose your own text adventure game. I then really began programming with Java, where I made simple applications like a web crawler, a recreation of the classic game "Snake", and more. I eventually began working in C#, where I created an edge detection program, and started working on a personal assistant (akin to Cortana or Siri).

Later on, I picked up web development for professional work, and over time became enthralled with everything web development related. I work on several projects in Ruby on Rails, have dabbled in some front end javascript frameworks like EmberJS and NodeJS, and have worked on tons of websites and projects in HTML and CSS augmented by excessive amounts of jQuery (such as this very site).

I have also picked up lots of skills in game development, from 3D modeling in Blender, to working with the Unity Engine to create several different games and visualizations. These skills have also have ended up serving me well in the creation of ocassional computer generated art pieces I've made for fun.

With some friends I also started up indigoBox Studios, a Chicago software collective that focuses on bringing people together to collaborate on solving technological problems. We make a wide variety of wild things, depending on what different members propose that we should do. If you want to learn more about indigoBox, go to our website.

These days I have become keenly interested in user experience design and improving how people interact with websites and technology. In my free time, I program (just as much as I do at work), play video games, drink chai lattes and expensive tea, and am always listening to music.
